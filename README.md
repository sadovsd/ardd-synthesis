# ARDD Synthesis

A personal knowledge base built from the Aging Research and Drug Discovery (ARDD) YouTube channel — notes, interpretations, and connections drawn from the talks and the papers behind them.

## What this is

Part notebook, part second brain. Notes are not just summaries but interpretations, critiques, and attempts to connect ideas across speakers, papers, and disciplines.

## Goals

- Distill key arguments and evidence from each talk in my own words
- Read the papers behind the talks, not just the abstracts
- Connect ideas across speakers — where they agree, disagree, or talk past each other
- Build a methods reference covering both computational and biological techniques
- Surface open questions worth exploring further

## Structure

- `talks/` — Notes on individual ARDD talks, grouped by speaker
- `methods/computational/` — Stats, modeling, and ML approaches
- `methods/biological/` — Assays, model organisms, wet lab methods
- `synthesis/` — Cross-talk insights, connections, and open questions

## Current focus

Fedichev and the biophysics of aging — stochastic dynamics, criticality, and what physics-based models reveal about the aging process. See `talks/fedichev/`.
