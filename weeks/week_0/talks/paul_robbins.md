# Paul Robbins at ARDD2023 — Novel senotherapeutic approaches to extend healthspan

Talk: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mp--nAWinhw

## Concepts

- Senolytics is about targeting senescent/dead cells.
- **Markers of senescence** used to quantify treatment effects: p16, p21, IL-1β, IL-6.

## Key ideas

- `Ercc1`-deficient mouse — a severe form of progeria (this also showed up in an earlier talk this week — see [hoeijmakers.md](hoeijmakers.md)).
- Muscle-derived stem cell injection helps the `Ercc1 -/-` mice.
- Incubating the good cells with the bad/old cells makes them soak up the good stuff and regain good differentiation — like parabiosis.
- **Extracellular vesicles (EVs)** confer the good effect. They asked: what components of the EVs actually confer this effect? Their hypothesis (without a lot of evidence yet) was that it's the **microRNA composition**.
- A certain combination of miRNAs had the best effect.
- They asked what the miRNAs target — 3 of them target transcription factors that act on **p53**.

## Background

- **IMR-90** is a widely used human diploid fibroblast cell line isolated from normal lung tissue of a 16-week-old female fetus. Because they have a stable, normal genome and a reliable lifespan of ~58 population doublings, they're an essential research tool.

## Future directions / open questions

- Why and how is the miRNA effect **combinatorial** — why does a combination outperform individual miRNAs?
