There's a second, subtler reason that they don't lean on but matters: blood counts respond fast. Inflammation, stress, an infection — these move CBC on a timescale of days to weeks, which is exactly the window where you'd expect to see a "bounce back" and measure recovery. Methylation is a slower, more stable mark; it's not obvious it even fluctuates on a weekly scale the way the Langevin picture needs. So even if you had dense DNAm data, it's an open question whether the same fast wobble-and-recover dynamics would show up.
